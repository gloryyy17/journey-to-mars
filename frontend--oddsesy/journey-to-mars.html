Captain's Log
Day 001 — Departure

Earth is still visible from the observation deck. A perfect blue marble receding slowly into the dark. Yara says she won't look back after today. I understand why.

🌍
Day 047 — Deep Space

Reactor B is running 2% hotter than nominal. Kenji spent six hours in the maintenance shaft. His solution: percussive maintenance. It worked. Log entry over.

🔧
Day 089 — Mid-Transit

Dr. Patel discovered an irregularity in the soil sample protocols. Recalibrated the spectrometers for the fourth time. The research never sleeps, even 100 million km from the lab.

🔬
Day 134 — Asteroid Belt

Navigation routed us through a sparse corridor. Three rocks larger than the ship passed within 40,000 km. Beautiful. Terrifying. The silence between them is absolute.

☄️
Day 178 — Mars Visible

First visual confirmation — a rust-coloured point of light, brighter than any star. The crew gathered at the observation window and said nothing for a long time. Nothing needed to be said.

🔴
Day 210 — Orbit Insertion

Orbital burn complete. We are in Mars orbit. Below us, Jezero Crater. Tomorrow we descend. The weight of what this means settles like Martian dust — quietly, everywhere, all at once.
